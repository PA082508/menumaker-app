# E-Forms for Directors

Families fill our forms on their own phone and sign with a finger. You send them a
link or a QR code; the signed form lands in your Children inbox for review. Nothing is
printed, mailed, or re-typed.

This guide covers what you do. It takes about five minutes to read.

---

## 1. Send a family their packet

**Children → Add Child.** Pick the set that matches the child:

| Set | Use it for |
|---|---|
| **Starter** | A brand-new family. The short set that begins enrollment. |
| **Toddler / Preschool** | A child aged 18 months and up. |
| **Infants** | A child under 18 months. Adds the infant forms. |

Each set opens with its forms already ticked — the ones that set requires. You can
untick anything the family has already given you.

Then:

- **Open packet ↗** — opens what the family will see, so you can check it first.
- **Copy link** — paste it into a text or e-mail.
- **QR** — tap the small code to enlarge it, then let the parent scan it off your
  screen. Nothing to print.

The link always carries your center. A family that opens it sees your center's name,
address and phone — not another center's.

## 2. Send the whole packet, or just one form

Above the list of forms there is one master tick:

> ☑ **Full packet — select every form**

Tap it and every form in the set is selected, including the ones marked *if
applicable*. Tap it again and the set returns to its **required** forms — the ones it
started with. It does not clear everything: a packet with no forms cannot be sent, so
there is nothing to undo by accident.

To send a single form instead — say a family only owes you the Income Eligibility
Application — leave that one ticked, untick the rest, and share the link or QR. The
parent sees only that form.

Each form also has **its own** small QR next to it. That one always opens that single
form for your center. Use it when a parent is standing in front of you.

## 3. What the family sees

The link opens a simple checklist on their phone. Each card is one form, and each
card says what it is:

- **Fill & sign** — they complete it and sign with a finger. Numbered, so they know
  how many are left.
- **Keep for your records** — a document to download or print. No signature. The
  Parent Handbook is one of these; each center has its own.
- **Director provides** — you will hand this over in person. Nothing for them to do
  online.

The **first** form is always the Consent. Once a parent signs it, every later form in
the packet offers a **"✍️ Use my signature"** button that stamps the same signature in
one tap. They draw once, not eight times. If they would rather sign each one by hand,
they still can.

As they finish each form, its card gets a ✓. They can close the page and come back.

## 4. Review what comes in

**Children → Enrollment.** The red number is how many are waiting for you.

Open a submission and you see exactly what the parent filled in, with their
signature. Check it against what you know, then **Approve** — the child moves to your
roster — or **Reject** with a reason, which tells the family what to fix.

Staff submissions never appear here. They have their own door: **Staff → Enrollment**.

## 5. New employees

**Staff → Add Staff** works the same way as Add Child: a link and a QR for the
onboarding packet.

The employee signs the **Staff Consent** first — that is what lets them reuse their
signature — and then the **Staff Enrollment**. Same "sign once" behaviour as families.

Their signature and a parent's never mix, even on the same tablet.

## If something looks wrong

- **A parent says the link asks them to "open this packet from your center's link"** —
  they were sent a link that lost its center. Send them a fresh one from Add Child.
- **A form shows "Coming soon"** — that form is not live for your center yet. The
  family cannot fill it and does not need to.
- **A QR opens an old version of a form** — it should not: every QR points at the
  packet page, which looks up the current version each time it opens. Tell us if you
  see it.

## What we never ask you to do

- Print a packet to hand out — unless you want to.
- Re-type anything a parent already typed.
- Chase a signature twice.
