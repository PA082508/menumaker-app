# Meal Count

Meal Count records daily meal participation for CACFP reimbursement. Every child
present at every meal must be counted accurately. Milk and portions are calculated
automatically from each child's age (date of birth).

> ⚠️ **CACFP compliance.** Meal counts are the foundation of your reimbursement
> claim. Counting absent children, missing meals, or entering counts late can cause
> claim disallowances and audit findings. **Count only children who are present and
> eating the meal.**

## For teachers

Check present children for each meal — about 30 seconds.

1. **Select your center and classroom.** At the top, confirm your center is selected, then choose your classroom tab.
2. **Select the meal** — Breakfast, AM Snack, Lunch, or Supper. The current meal is highlighted by time of day.
3. **Select the day.** Click today's column (Mon–Fri for the current week).
4. **Check each child present.** Check the box next to every child who is present **and** eating this meal. Do **not** check absent children or those who brought their own food.
5. **Milk is automatic.** Milk type and ounces are set by each child's age. Special diets/allergies are noted next to the name.
6. **Submit.** When all present children are checked, the count saves automatically; the checked count updates live.
7. **Repeat for each meal.** Every meal and day is recorded separately.

> 💡 The count takes less time than signing a paper sheet and saves straight to CACFP records.

## For cooks

Use **Kitchen View** for planning and weekly totals.

1. **Open Kitchen View** (Operations → Kitchen View) — combined totals for all classrooms, by meal and day.
2. **Check the totals** — total children, breakdown by age group, and milk totals in cups.
3. **Week View** — the full week at a glance for food planning and ordering.
4. **Kitchen Report** (Operations → Kitchen Report) — print/export the weekly summary.
5. **Export for Google Sheets** — the Export button (top right) downloads the data for CACFP reporting.

## For directors

Review counts and prepare CACFP site claim reports.

1. **Director View** — top-right of Meal Count, switch to the summary across all classrooms.
2. **Review counts** — verify every classroom submitted for each meal (missing counts show as 0/empty).
3. **Week View** — review the full week before submitting; totals must match your site claim report.
4. **Export for Google Sheets** — CACFP-ready format.
5. **Site Claim Report** (Reports → Site Claim) — the monthly CACFP report from the month's counts.

## CACFP rules

- **Count only present children** — physically present **and** eating; never absent or own-lunch children.
- **Count at meal time** — counts must reflect actual participation, entered at the meal.
- **Milk is by age** — set from date of birth. Infants: formula (not counted in CACFP milk); ages 1–2: whole milk 4 oz; ages 2+: 1% milk.
- **No guessing** — unsure if a child is present? Check with the teacher before submitting.
- **Same-day entry** — enter on the day of service; correct prior days only with your director.
- **Staff meals** — tracked separately on the Staff tab in the classroom view.
